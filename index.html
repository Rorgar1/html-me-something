Adventures in Scandinavia

  • Kristianstad
  • Lund, Sweden
  • Malmo
  • Copenhagen

In March, 2018, I had the opportunity to accompany my husband on a business trip to Sweden and Denmark. I knew nothing about Sweden except the meatballs and fish. Scandinavia in March is cold, and gray, but still has a bleak sort of beauty. One understands how the Vikings became so fierce. The people of Sweden today are still slow to warm up to strangers, but once you make a connection, they are among the most welcoming people I have met.
By the end of the trip, Scandinavia became my new favorite vacation destination.

Kristianstad, Sweden

Kristianstad is a tiny town with a long history. We spent a week here while my husband worked. I explored the shops, museum, and many coffeehouses. In Sweden, there is something called "fika", when one sits down over a coffee and sweet roll to stop and enjoy life, and maybe read a book or newspaper for a few hours. Fika is my new favorite meal.
I was surprised that everyone I met chose to speak English rather than Swedish. Even the signs were written in English and Swedish, so it was easy to feel right at home.

Lund, Sweden

About an hour from the big city of Copenhagen is the city of Lund, known for its university and the historic Kulturen museum. Kulturen has architectural examples from the days when roofs were still covered with grass, up through to the last century.

There are also Viking skulls. Can't go to Scandinavia without seeing Viking skulls.

Malmo, Sweden

Malmo is just across a bridge from Copenhagen. But it is most definitely Swedish, not Danish, though Denmark once claimed it. Malmohus is the old castle and now museum.

Malmo is even more beautiful in the snow. Yes, it snows in March. Spring doesn't arrive until mid-April, but there is nothing like a snowy day to enjoy fika!

Copenhagen, Denmark

Did you know Denmark still has a royal family? It is one of the longest-reigning families in the world! Buckingham Palace has nothing on Amalienborg Palace. It is still the royal home, a museum, a historical building, and takes a full day to explore. There is even a changing-of-the-guard ceremony!

Copenhagen is a harbor city, and it has canals that run through it. It is the Venice of Scandinavia.

Copenhagen is also the home of the famed writer of fairy tales, Hans Christian Andersen. His well-known Little Mermaid sits on a boulder in the harbor, waiting for her prince.

Copenhagen also has the strangest coffeehouse we had ever been to. In a cute little place, where every surface covered in lace doilies and Danish flags, we took a break to relax.
Then we looked up....

After two weeks, Scandinavia wove it's charm on us. The people, the places, the history, the coffee (even the creepy coffeehouse had delicious coffee and pastries), the ease of getting around. I would go back to Scandinavia in a heartbeat. Maybe next time in summer, though.
